01

The three laws

Break one and it stops reading as STRATA.
/ 01

Amber is the only "active" colour

The accent means in-progress / focused / the one thing that matters — and it means that on every screen. Never two meanings at once.

/ 02

Mono is reserved for data

Every number, code, timestamp, and status is IBM Plex Mono. Prose never is. It's how the eye separates data from language at a glance.

/ 03

Depth is luminance, not shadow

ink → panel → panel-hover is one flat step up, plus a 1px hairline. No shadows, gradients, or glassmorphism. A console flush-mounts its panels.

Buttons — the accent is a signal, not a default CTA

Ghost is the default; the amber fill appears at most once per screen; danger borrows the streak rose. The stamp (260ms, slight overshoot) is the one signature motion — fired once when an objective lands complete.

06

Do & don't

Do

  • Treat amber as the only "active" colour — one meaning per screen.
  • Keep mono for data; reserve emerald for "complete" alone.
  • Pair every status colour with text — never colour-only.
  • Number only what is genuinely sequential.
  • Use real <button>s with a visible focus ring.

Don't

  • Spend the accent on two meanings on one screen.
  • Let body text drift into mono — the hierarchy collapses.
  • Reach for shadows, gradients, or glassmorphism for depth.
  • Add a global numbering scheme to non-sequential topics.
  • Let Fraunces creep past titles and rank callouts.
